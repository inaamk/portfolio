I’m a cybersecurity graduate with a dual academic background in Computer Science and Cybersecurity — but more importantly, I’m a builder, a problem-solver, and a self-driven learner. My passion for technology began early and grew without shortcuts: before even knowing what frameworks were, I was already building Android apps and websites from scratch, teaching myself how systems work — not because I had to, but because I couldn’t stop exploring.

Over six years as an IT specialist in a public school, I gained practical experience in resolving complex issues, securing digital environments, and supporting staff through technology. That role wasn’t just a job — it became the launchpad for my deeper mission: to protect systems, data, and people through cybersecurity.

I later earned a Master’s in Cybersecurity at EPITA, France, where I immersed myself in real-world security domains — threat detection, vulnerability analysis, cryptography, and SOC operations. But most of what I know, I earned through building. I developed projects like AutoThreat, an alert enrichment framework powered by threat intelligence (OTX, AbuseIPDB), and integrated ELK Stack with Zeek and Suricata for real-time log analysis and alert correlation.

To give back, I launched a bilingual LinkedIn series, SOC Level 1 Essentials, where I break down key SOC concepts to help other aspiring analysts. My learning is continuous, hands-on, and shared — not just studied.

Cybersecurity, for me, is not a trend. It's a commitment to securing digital ecosystems with precision, ethics, and intelligence. I bring not only technical skill but lived experience, resilience, and a mindset shaped by building from the ground up.
